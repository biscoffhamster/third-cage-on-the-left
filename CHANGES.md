# Editorial Changes: PDF to Website

This document records all corrections and editorial decisions made when adapting
*Third Cage on the Left* from the original PDF to the website version.

The source PDF covers pages 1–10 (with multiple parts per page) and was written
during the COVID-19 lockdown in 2020 as a series of posts. The website condenses
this into a structured book with eight chapters and an appendix.

## Editorial Omissions

The PDF was written as a series of daily lockdown posts and contains preambles,
personal reflections, and digressions that were not carried through to the final
book form. The following passages were intentionally removed or condensed.

### Lockdown context / preambles
Each "page" of the PDF began with a personal preamble written to friends and
family during the 2020 COVID-19 lockdown. These were removed throughout, as they
are diary-like entries rather than part of the memoir narrative.

### Preface
- Removed: An eBay pricing aside — "(You could buy a vintage one today on eBay for £200.)" — from the Automaticket machines sentence.

### Father's Journey to Africa
- Condensed: Opening rewritten from "My first two posts were my childhood memories until I was six, but at this point I will answer a question often asked…" to "I will now answer a question often asked:".
- Removed: "(Sounds familiar?)" aside after reference to the Great Depression.
- Removed: "In addition to Oxford, Edingburgh, London and Bangor offered degrees in forestry." (the sentence lists rival universities; "Edingburgh" was also a typo for "Edinburgh" in the PDF).

### The Gold Coast Years
- Removed: Extended personal story about Richard using his handful of Twi phrases with a lift attendant at the Houses of Parliament.
- Condensed: Pages 3 parts 2–4 of the PDF (which describe George's wartime service, his marriage on the first day of the Blitz, the invisible-ink interception, his role supervising wartime furniture workshops, and how a friendship with an Imperial Airways manager enabled animal shipments) were condensed into a shorter "Gold Coast Years" chapter. A cousin's written account of this period was also omitted.

### The Ostrich Story
- Removed: Editor's self-referential aside — "Editor's note. I prefer the Pidgin version."
- Removed: Steward's parenthetical aside — "(I think you are getting hang of it now.)"

### Mr. Ezra and Mandarins
- Removed: Extended description of seeing Mandarin Duck sightings build up over several years around Hartburn and Kielder.
- Removed: Background research on Alfred Ezra, including the Wikipedia link and Sassoon family connection.
- Removed: "In future chapters I will talk about some of the other characters I encountered."
- Removed: Introductory paragraphs linking back to the Africa chapters and describing the house in the zoo (hippos and giraffes as neighbours).

### The Duke of Bedford and Some Fishy Tales
- Removed: Sentence about a friend who googled Mr. Ezra after reading a previous post.
- Removed: Extended passage about Miss Nobel's parrot collection and an aside about sexing Amazon parrots by endoscopy.
- Removed: Extended passage about the 12th Duke of Bedford — including details about his far-right politics and the circumstances of his death in a shooting accident.
- Removed: "They were interesting times." after the Zander fishing story.
- Removed: The subplot of catching two small goldfish from Woburn's ornamental pond for the family garden in London.

### Pets at Home
- Removed: "In Finland they run ahead of hunters and bark at base of trees into which game birds have sought sanctuary." (description of the Finnish Spitz's hunting use).
- Removed: "For a while the second Blue Peter tortoise, George, also lived with us. After the fishing expedition to the Woburn Chinese Garden pond, the two anonymous goldfish joined the family."
- Removed: Stories about the au pair who was terrified of animals, the au pair who took a bear cub for a walk on Finchley Road, a lion cub staying over a weekend, and a budgie that turned out not to be Smokey.
- Removed: "Pets Leaving Home" section (PDF page 8 part 2 and beyond).

- Removed: Story about cutting a finger while repairing a window.
- Removed: Passage about the Allen Rototscythe mower and its missing silencer.

### Appendix: George Cansdale Obituary
The obituary in the PDF is the full text as published in *The Independent*. The website
carries an abridged version. The following passages were omitted:
- Passage about fingers covered in Elastoplast and handling the most dangerous animals.
- Extra years studying for his B Sc and training for the Colonial Service.
- Story of a friend from Paignton Zoo who asked him to bring home specimens; the 80-specimen first collection (squirrels, hornbills, owls, touracos, snakes).
- The burrowing viper incident in which Cansdale was bitten through the index finger, which eventually rotted to nearly half an inch short.
- Teams of boys trained to collect specimens; West African "firsts" including two new species (a bat and a flying squirrel), eight snakes not previously recorded for the Gold Coast, and a record-length black cobra.
- Details of his marriage to Sheila Williamson and their shared Christian faith (Bible study group, Churchwarden of All Souls, Crusaders Union).
- Details of being headhunted by the Zoological Society in 1947, and of Dr Gwynne Vevers' retirement.
- Detailed account of the turnstile fraud (the man with a palatial house in Bedford, estimate that 10% of income was being stolen, fuchsia-selling keeper).
- Outside broadcast from the zoo in which Wynford Vaughan Thomas was bitten by a large Indian gecko.
- Radio contributions to Children's Hour.
- Historical context about 1990s wildlife programming and Attenborough's full 1992 AGM presidential address quote.
- Full list of books written (Animals of West Africa, Animals and Man, George Cansdale's Zoo Book, Belinda the Bushbaby, Reptiles of West Africa, Behind the Scenes at a Zoo, West African Snakes, Animals of Bible Lands) and the research behind Animals of Bible Lands.
- 1960s directorial roles with Morecambe Marine Land, Chessington Zoo, and Natureland Skegness.
- Origin of SWF Filtration Ltd (pioneering clean water through beach sand, then adapted for developing countries), and the IBM/World Business Award for Sustainable Development in 1990.
- Cottage in Essex as a magnet for village children; the Blue Peter studio rota with nine as the magic age; pre-visit phone calls offering produce, chutney and marmalade.
- Unlucky fall in 1991 and fractured femur; letter to the Telegraph criticising Grade I listings of the Penguin Pool and Gorilla House.
- Loss of part of a foot in the 1980s from skin cancer contracted during years in the tropics.
- Death of Sheila in March 1992 and George's devoted nursing of her; his subsequent independent life including support for Inter-Care charity.
